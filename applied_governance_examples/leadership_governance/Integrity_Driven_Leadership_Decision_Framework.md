## Scope
This framework governs **leadership decision-making related to people**, including:
- Performance appraisal
- Task and responsibility allocation
- Role assignment
- Recognition and corrective actions

It is designed to ensure **fairness, transparency, integrity, and accountability**, while minimizing bias, favoritism, and undocumented judgment.

This framework is **non-regulatory** but auditable and defensible.

---

## 1. Purpose

To ensure leadership decisions are:
- Evidence-informed
- Ethically grounded
- Clearly communicated
- Properly documented
- Open to review and feedback

The framework explicitly addresses risks related to **bias, opacity, and misuse of authority** in people decisions.

---

## 2. Decision Formation

### Objective
Ensure the decision is deliberate, unbiased, and justifiable.

### Governance Controls
- Decision origin identified:
  - Individual decision-maker, or
  - Collaborative decision with relevant stakeholders or authorities
- Alternative viewpoints considered:
  - Arguments for and against the decision
- Viable options identified and evaluated
- Decision basis explicitly stated as:
  - Verifiable facts and data, or
  - Experience and intuition (explicitly acknowledged)
- Supporting evidence identified where applicable:
  - Performance metrics
  - KPIs
  - Feedback records
  - Workload or contribution data
- Decision documentation status:
  - Formally documented (yes/no)
  - Location and ownership of documentation identified
- Existence of discussion records:
  - Meeting notes
  - Email trails
  - Decision or approval logs

---

## 3. Communication with Stakeholders

### Objective
Maintain transparency, alignment, and trust.

### Governance Controls
- Decision communicated to all relevant stakeholders
- Decision criteria and rationale clearly articulated
- Feedback or dissent:
  - Actively acknowledged
  - Recorded where raised
- Confirmation of shared understanding, even where disagreement persists

Transparency is prioritized over consensus.

---

## 4. Communication with the Decision Receiver

### Objective
Ensure clarity, dignity, and traceability in delivery.

### Governance Controls
- Mode of communication identified:
  - Verbal
  - Written
  - Formal meeting
- Rationale and evaluation criteria clearly explained
- Communication evidence:
  - Email, system entry, meeting minutes
  - If verbal, a written summary or follow-up record exists

Communication must preserve **respect, clarity, and accountability**.

---

## 5. Addressing the Receiver’s Response

### Objective
Prevent silent dissatisfaction and unmanaged escalation.

### Governance Controls
- Receiver response identified:
  - Acceptance
  - Contestation
- If contested:
  - Concerns actively solicited
  - Concerns documented
- Evaluation of concerns:
  - Reviewed objectively
  - Addressed with reasoning
- Follow-up actions:
  - Clarifications
  - Adjustments
  - Reconsideration
- Outcomes recorded for traceability

Disagreement is treated as governance input, not defiance.

---

## 6. Documentation & Accountability

### Objective
Create an auditable and defensible decision trail.

### Governance Controls
- Documentation exists for each decision stage
- Stakeholders involved are explicitly listed
- Supporting evidence stored in a centralized location
- Decision authority clearly identified
- Final sign-off owner recorded
- Decision alignment verified against:
  - Organizational policies
  - Ethical standards
  - Performance management guidelines

Accountability is explicit, not implied.

---

## 7. Review & Continuous Improvement

- Decisions may be reviewed periodically or upon escalation
- Patterns of bias, disputes, or dissatisfaction are analyzed
- Framework effectiveness is reassessed and refined over time
