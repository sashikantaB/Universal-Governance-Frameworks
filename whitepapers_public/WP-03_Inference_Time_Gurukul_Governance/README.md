# White Paper III
## Inference-Time Gurukul Governance  
### A Meta-Cognitive Architecture for Continuous AI Alignment

**Author:** Sashikanta Barik  
**Status:** Draft / Conceptual

**Domain:** AI Governance, Responsible AI, Ethical Alignment, Systems Architecture

---

## Overview

This white paper introduces the **Inference-Time Gurukul Governance Architecture**, extending the training-time alignment framework of WP-02 (Dharma-Gate) into **continuous, inference-time supervision**.

The architecture embeds a **Guru Gate supervisory model** that monitors task-oriented Shishya agents in real time, referencing an immutable ethical core (Shastra Layer). Misaligned outputs are intercepted, corrected, and logged for audit and governance intelligence analysis.

---

## Core Thesis

> **Ethical alignment cannot end at training.  
> AI outputs must be continuously supervised and aligned during deployment.**

Gurukul Governance reframes AI oversight as **operational, meta-cognitive, and enforceable**, rather than post-hoc or advisory.

---

## Key Contributions

- **Shastra Layer:** Immutable ethical axioms provide a consistent alignment baseline  
- **Guru AI:** Meta-cognitive supervisor performing real-time audits, approvals, and interventions  
- **Shishya AI:** Task-oriented agents operating under strict supervisory constraints  
- **Inference-Time Governance:** Extends Dharma-Gate principles to live operations  
- **Governance Intelligence Plane:** Logs and analyzes misalignments for continuous improvement

---

## Why This Paper Matters

Most governance frameworks stop at training or rely on human oversight. WP-03 explains **how to operationalize governance during inference**, ensuring that autonomous or agentic AI systems:

- Remain aligned with ethical principles  
- Produce auditable, compliant outputs  
- Scale safely without human bottlenecks  

This paper sets the foundation for future operational white papers such as the **Automated Governance Pipeline (AGP)**.
