## Deployment Governance: Post-Deployment Accountability and Risk-Aligned System Hardening

**Author:** Sashikanta Barik  
**Date:** January 2026  
**Domain:** AI Governance • Ethical AI • Post-Deployment Safety • Risk-Aligned Oversight

---

## Overview

This white paper introduces a **post-deployment governance model** for AI systems.  
It emphasizes continuous monitoring, risk-aligned intervention, and audit-ready accountability after deployment. By structuring the "in-production" lifecycle as a governed process, the framework ensures system robustness, ethical compliance, and stakeholder trust.

---

## Core Thesis

> Safe AI systems are not only trained responsibly; they must be monitored and governed after deployment to prevent operational harm and misalignment.

Deployment Governance (DG) transforms the post-deployment phase into a structured, ethical hardening process, reducing the fragile "honeymoon period" into a controlled, auditable, and accountable workflow.

---

## Key Contributions

- **Post-Deployment Monitoring:** Continuous observation of system behavior and anomalies  
- **Risk-Aligned Intervention:** Tiered escalation based on operational and societal impact  
- **Audit-Ready Logging:** Comprehensive logging of interventions, metrics, and governance actions  
- **Ethical Hardening:** Feedback loops ensure alignment with ethical and regulatory standards  
- **Compliance Alignment:** Supports EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, and Zero-Trust operational assumptions  

---

## Why This Paper Matters

- Minimizes downstream harm from deployed AI systems  
- Enhances organizational accountability and governance maturity  
- Enables early detection of misalignment and unsafe behaviors  
- Provides a repeatable, auditable process for operational AI systems
