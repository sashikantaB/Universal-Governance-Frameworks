## Scope
This framework governs the **end-to-end development lifecycle** of a badminton player — from raw talent identification to elite performance — by applying structured governance,
risk management, and continuous improvement principles.

This is a **non-regulatory, performance-governance framework**.

---

## 1. Aim

To systematically convert **raw athletic talent into a world-class badminton player** by balancing performance excellence, physical and mental wellbeing, and long-term sustainability.

The framework ensures:
- Structured development
- Stakeholder accountability
- Risk-aware training
- Injury and burnout prevention
- Continuous performance evaluation

---

## 2. Stakeholder Governance Model

| Stakeholder         | Responsibility                                           |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| **Mother**          | Diet, nutrition habits, sleep routines                   |
| **Father**          | Logistics, equipment, transport, financial planning      |
| **Coach**           | Training design, skill development, performance strategy |
| **Physio / Doctor** | Injury prevention, recovery, physical & mental health    |

Clear role separation avoids overlap and accountability gaps.

---

## 3. Risk Discovery & Stakeholder Assessment

Risk assessment is conducted through structured interaction with all stakeholders:

- **Mother**
  - Sleep patterns
  - Nutrition habits
  - Interests and motivation levels

- **Father**
  - Financial constraints
  - Travel and commute challenges
  - Equipment availability

- **Coach**
  - Physical limitations
  - Stamina and workload tolerance
  - Skill learning speed and execution
  - Temperament and match psychology

- **Physio / Doctor**
  - Medical history
  - Special physical conditions
  - Injury risks
  - Psychological limitations, if any

Based on inputs, risks are categorized by severity.

---

## 4. Primary Risk Identification

| Risk              | Impact |
|-------------------|--------|
| Mental burnout    | High   |
| Physical burnout  | High   |
| Injury            | High   | 
| Demotivation      | Medium |
| Overtraining bias | Medium |

Burnout (mental and physical) is treated as a **critical systemic risk**.

---

## 5. Pre-Processing & Player Classification

- Assess current skill and fitness level
- Assign player to appropriate group:
  - Beginner
  - Intermediate
  - Advanced

This prevents:
- Performance bias
- Early overexposure
- Confidence damage due to mismatched competition

---

## 6. Training & Development Controls

### 6.1 Physical Training
- Strength and conditioning
- Stamina-building exercises
- Injury-prevention routines

### 6.2 Court Training
- Mobility
- Agility
- Footwork efficiency

### 6.3 Stroke Training
- Technique learning
- Shot accuracy
- Tactical awareness

### 6.4 Match Practice
- Applying learned techniques
- Decision-making under pressure

---

## 7. Observation & Rule Adherence

- Continuous observation during training and matches
- Repeated mistakes are documented
- Corrections are introduced through training adjustments

Mandatory adherence to:
- Court rules
- Game rules
- Assigned diet plan
- Training schedules

---

## 8. Testing & Stress Testing

### Testing
- Weekly internal matches
- Coach records weaknesses and progress
- Training plans updated accordingly

### Stress Testing
- Matches against more experienced players or coaches
- Performance under pressure observed and documented

---

## 9. Monitoring & Continuous Governance Loop

- Tournament matches monitored and reviewed
- Mistakes and patterns analyzed post-match
- Corrective actions integrated into next training cycle

This is a **continuous improvement loop**, not a one-time assessment.

---

## 10. Risk Mitigation Strategies

| Risk         | Mitigation Action                           |
|--------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Burnout      | Weekly rest periods and fun sessions        |
| Demotivation | Internal academy matches with incentives    |
| Injury       | Predefined rehabilitation and recovery plan |
| Overtraining | Training load recalibration                 |

Mitigation actions are proactive, not reactive.

---

## 11. Evaluation Metrics

| Metric            | Measurement                                  |
|-------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Stamina           | Number of continuous matches without fatigue |
| Endurance         | Maximum rally duration                       |
| Match performance | Wins vs losses                               |
| Competitive gap   | Point difference in matches                  |
| Consistency       | Performance stability across tournaments     |

---

## 12. Documentation & Records

### Regulations
- Court rules
- Academy rules
- Game rules

### Policies
- Diet policy
- Training policy
- Risk mitigation policy

### Records
- Mistake and correction logs
- Injury records
- Rehabilitation and recovery timelines

Documentation supports **learning, not punishment**.

---

## 13. Review & Evolution

- Framework reviewed periodically
- Adjusted based on player growth and competition level
- Updated after injuries, burnout signals, or performance plateaus
